I'm going to talk a lot about myself, to provide concrete examples and not just ideas that are not actionable.

How it started

  • Access astronomical data programatically
  • Have them available in Ruby
  • Explain them
  • Make them accessible

Doubts

  • Who needs this?
  • Is it worth the effort?
  • This is hard and I'm not a real scientist (imposer syndrom)
    • Example: geocentric to topocentric coordinates is way more complex than anticipated
    • Example: turning barycentric vectors into topocentric coordinates, how am I supposed to know how to do that?
  • I could spend this time working on business ideas instead
  • Before ephemeris: results are just not good enough to compete, what's the point?
  • Lots of effort to share and no feedback (new releases and blog posts that got no attention)

Staying motivated

  • Passion: it's easy to spend time on something that gots you excited
  • Personal mission and challenge: Personal goals emerged as the project evolved (will to succeed, to learn, to share)
  • Small quantity of project management: goals, celebrate wins, talk about it
    • "Just like I wanted to lose weight, by telling others I started eating healthier, I was more likely to stick to it. Well, it didn't work, but it was worth trying."
  • Examples from the community: Faker gem, started almost 7 years ago, initially a very niche need, now a very popular gem with hundreds of individual contributors, thousands of stars, used for testing, seeding, development, fun and more.

Niche can also be useful

Because nobody's expecting anything, you can take your time, try things, make mistakes, put attention to details (nice API, tests).
